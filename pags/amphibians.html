Frogs

The red-eyed tree frog is one of the most striking and surely we have been able to see it on more than one occasion in animal reports. What you may not have been so clear about is that you can keep them at home as pets and that they are one of the easiest amphibian species to care for.

It belongs to the Hylidae family that is made up of 850 species that live on 5 continents. On this occasion, the red-eyed tree frog has its origin in the American continent.

Salamanders

A salamander is an amphibian and is characterized by its slender body, short nose, and long tail. Salamanders are clawless and most species have four toes on their front feet and five on their hind feet. They range in length from a little over an inch (2.5 centimeters) to about 5 feet (152 centimeters).

Most salamanders need to keep their skin moist and therefore must be around water. Like other vertebrates, salamanders can regrow lost limbs and other body parts. Reading these tips on caring for salamanders will help you keep them healthy and happy as pets.

Newts

The fire-bellied newt (cynops pirroghaster) makes a terrific pet for any amphibian lover. They're adorable, easy to find, and affordable. In addition, these animals become great pets if they have an aquarium adapted to their needs. Remember that they can live a long time (from five to ten years, some even reach thirty years) so you must do everything in your power to ensure that your new companion has a safe and healthy environment.

Toads

Toads or Bufonidae as they are scientifically known, are currently one of the most widely purchased exotic animals as pets due to their easy reproduction and care.

In addition, you can find them almost anywhere in the world, with the exception of arctic or polar areas, and unlike other amphibians in captivity, their acquisition does not usually cause legal problems. You can also find them more and more commonly in pet stores according to your location.
