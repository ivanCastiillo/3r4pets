Water turtles

Your new little friend is a cold-blooded animal and in order to regulate her body temperature, she needs her habitat to be as close as possible to her area of origin (Southeast United States and Northeast Mexico).

It is very important that you review the catalog of invasive species in your country so as not to contribute to the trade of unauthorized species and to comply with the legislation to protect the planet's biodiversity.

Iguanas

Iguanas are reptiles that originate from Central and South America, their habitat being characterized by warm temperatures and high humidity. Despite being one of the most popular pet reptiles in our country, there is still a certain lack of knowledge regarding the basic care they need to ensure their well-being and ensure the best quality of life.

They are herbivorous animals and their diet should be varied and rich in calcium, with leafy vegetables such as chard, lamb's lettuce, romaine lettuce, spinach, watercress being a fundamental part of their diet.

Chamaleons

Chameleons are arboreal reptiles, that is, they live in the branches of trees. Therefore, you will have to make sure that your cage for chameleons has enough space to install several branches with abundant foliage for them to climb and hide. A small chameleon will need a cage of at least 50x50x90 cm with three wire walls to ensure good ventilation. Remember that chameleons need a humidity of almost 80%, so if the cage is not well ventilated, fungi and bacteria that are harmful to the health of your pet may appear. It is also important that you find a quiet place to place the cage, since chameleons are easily stressed, and away from cold drafts.

Geckos

The Leopard Gecko (Eublepharis macularius) is one of the most booming reptiles in recent years whose maintenance is relatively simple, being a reptile suitable for beginners. It is a nocturnal desert lizard of the geconid family (Gekkonidae) of small size (18-24 cm) that lives in the Middle East from Iran to Pakistan.

The first thing to keep in mind is that it is a nocturnal animal (hence its vertical slanted pupil) so it will be mainly active at the beginning of twilight or sunset and will spend most of the day hidden in its shelter sleeping.
